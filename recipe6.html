Ingredients

    For Panner:
  • 200-250 gms paneer (cubed)
  • 1-2 tbsp butter or oil (for frying paneer)
    • For Gravy:
  • 3-4 medium tomatoes (roughly chopped)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves (chopped)
  • 1 inch ginger (chopped)
  • 1-2 green chilies (optional)
  • 8-10 cashews
  • 1-2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-2 cardamom pods
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 2-3 tbsp fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

How to make Paneer Butter Masala

Prepare the Tomato-Cashew Paste: 

In a pan, heat a little butter and add chopped onions, ginger, garlic, green chilies, and cashews.
Saut√© until the onions turn translucent. Then, add the tomatoes and cook until soft.
Let this mixture cool and blend it into a smooth paste.
Fry Paneer (Optional):

Heat 1-2 tbsp butter in a pan, lightly fry the paneer cubes until golden on the edges. Remove and set aside.
Prepare the Gravy:

In the same pan, heat 1 tbsp butter. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves.
Add the prepared tomato-cashew paste and cook for 5-7 minutes on medium heat.
Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
Add Paneer:

Add 1/2 to 1 cup of water (depending on how thick you want the gravy) and bring it to a boil.
Stir in the fried paneer cubes and kasuri methi. Cook for 3-4 minutes on low heat.
Finish with Cream:

Add fresh cream to the gravy and stir gently. Cook for another 2 minutes.
Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Serve Paneer Butter Masala with naan, roti, or steamed rice. Enjoy!
