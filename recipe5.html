Ingredients

For the Rice:
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4-5 green cardamom
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt, to taste
  • Water for boiling
For the Chicken Marinade:
  • 500g chicken (bone-in or boneless)
  • Â½ cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon biryani masala or garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
For Assembling:
2 onions, thinly sliced (for fried onions)
2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or oil
1 teaspoon saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
1 tablespoon rose water or kewra water (optional)

How to make Chicken Dum Biryani!

1. Prepare the Rice:
>>Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch.
>>Boil water in a large pot, adding salt, bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon.
>>Once the water boils, add the rice and cook until it is 70-80% done (it should still be firm). Drain the water and set the rice aside.
2. Marinate the Chicken:
>>In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, biryani masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, salt, green chilies, chopped coriander, mint leaves, and lemon juice.
>>Add the chicken pieces to this marinade, mix well, and let it sit for at least 1 hour (the longer, the better for flavor).
3. Fry the Onions:
>>Heat oil in a pan and fry the thinly sliced onions until they turn golden and crispy. Drain them on paper towels and set aside. These are your fried onions (Birista).
4. Assemble the Biryani:
>>In a heavy-bottomed pan, spread a layer of marinated chicken at the bottom.
>>Add half of the fried onions on top of the chicken.
>>Now, layer half of the parboiled rice over the chicken.
>>Sprinkle a little saffron milk, rose water (if using), chopped coriander, and mint leaves.
>>Add the remaining fried onions over the rice.
>>Repeat the process with another layer of chicken and then the rest of the rice.
>>Finally, drizzle with the remaining saffron milk, and dot with ghee on top.
5. Cook on Dum (Slow Cooking):
>>Seal the pot with a tight-fitting lid or cover with foil to trap the steam.
>>Cook on a low flame for 25-30 minutes. Alternatively, you can place a heavy pan or tawa below the biryani pot to avoid direct heat and cook on very low heat.
>>After 30 minutes, turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid.
6. Serve:
Gently fluff the biryani and serve hot with raita, boiled eggs, or salan (curry).

Enjoy your delicious Chicken Dum Biryani!
